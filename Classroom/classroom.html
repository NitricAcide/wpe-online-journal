Book Reading Activity

This book reading activity was held on September 25 to October 2, a whole week given to us to read a book that is related to anything in computer studies. I chose after reading the countless catalogues, "Low Tech Hacking". This was an activity to be done for the WPE subject to showcase the value of classroom in the 5 C's of Silliman, as well as equip us with skills in accessing the library for resources.

The book "Low Tech Hacking" is a book that basically explores the often overlooked vulnerabilities in security systems that doesn't always involve the use of advanced tools or technical expertise. Yet, the book also highlights the different ways in which these low tech methods can be used for ethical purposes, as well as granting awareness to these methods and how to combat them. It expresses the greater importaance of human factors that are involved in security so that we should be more vigilant and aware in what we do to keep ourselves secure.

This activity has taught me how to properly access the library and its resources, but moreso, it has given me a deeper appreciation for the value of reading and self learning. In this age of information overload, it's easy to get lost in the sea of content available online. However, taking the time to read a book allows for a more focused and immersive experience. It encourages critical thinking, reflection and a deeper understanding of specific subject matter. I'd say this activity has given me a better appreciation for the value of reading and self learning, as well as the importance of being able to access reliable resources through the library.

Because of this activity, I am more motivated to continue reading and exploring new topics, knowing that the library is a valuable resource that can aid in my journey of learning.

Mental Health Caravan

The Mental Health Caravan was an event that was advertised for freshmen to attend, featuring Dr. Carl Gerard Yap, a psychiatrist who'll be giving a lecture on builfing a mental health toolkit for the first year college students to prepare them for the college life. It was held on October 8 at the Luce auditorium.

During the Mental Health Caravan, I learned valuable insights on how to manage stress, self regulation and building resilience through the practical mental health tools that Dr. Yap has shared. He was able to be relatable and connect with students by his examples of students facing academic pressure, homesickness and procrastination. I think what was impactful to me was the mindfulness exercise, which gave us a few minutes of peace and focus inside the auditorium. It made me more mindful of slowing down and being present in the moment, showing importance of self-care and balanced and realize that mental wellness is not something selfish, but an essential part of growth and success in college.

This experience has showed me the importance about mental health. I used to think that managing stress was purle about avoiding pressure, but I learned that it's about facing challenges with healthy copying strategies. The Mental Health Caravan reminded me that resilience is built through small, consistent acts of care and self-awareness. I hope to apply what I've listened by being more mindful of my mental well-being and seek support when needed.

Because of this activity, I am more aware of my own mental health, more equipped with tools to handle the academic and life's pressures, and more committed to building resilience in myself and in those around me.
